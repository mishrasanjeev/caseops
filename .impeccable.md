# CaseOps — design context for the `impeccable` skill

> The `impeccable` skill at `.claude/skills/impeccable/SKILL.md` reads this
> file before any frontend work. Keep it short, specific, and opinionated —
> if a contributor can't name the reader in one sentence, the file is too
> vague.

## Target audience

- **Primary:** Indian legal professionals. Two flavours, with very different
  tolerances for UI novelty.
  - **Litigation partners and senior associates** in mid-sized Indian law
    firms (20–200 lawyers). Dense hours, high stakes, value information
    density over whitespace. Many reviewers on a shared Windows PC; some on
    iPad in court.
  - **General Counsels and legal ops** inside Indian corporates. Calmer
    workflow, boardroom audience, expect something that would sit comfortably
    beside Navan, Ironclad, or Harvey.
- **Secondary:** solo advocates on laptops at night. Fast connection not
  guaranteed; the interface must stay legible and fast on mid-range hardware.

## Use cases (top five)

1. Open the workspace in the morning, scan **hearings this week**, jump into
   one matter, produce a hearing pack in under an hour.
2. Draft a first pleading from a matter's documents, review with citations,
   and get partner sign-off before dispatch.
3. Review an inbound contract against a playbook, record obligations, and
   send redlines.
4. Decide which outside counsel to brief and track their spend against a
   budget.
5. Generate an explainable recommendation (forum / authority / next action),
   review it with citations intact, accept or defer.

Every one of these is **dense workflow**. The interface must never feel like
a marketing landing page inside the product.

## Brand personality / tone

Three concrete words: **considered, unflappable, court-ready**.

- **Considered.** We think before we speak. Nothing shouts, nothing
  pulses, nothing begs for attention with neon. A page is a well-prepared
  brief, not a nightclub.
- **Unflappable.** The UI stays calm under load — 500 invoices, 80 matters,
  3,000 activity events — without losing hierarchy.
- **Court-ready.** Typography has the authority of legal documents; density
  and alignment say "we take this seriously." Ornamental elements are out.

Forbidden tones: "playful", "fun", "cute", "delightful", "friendly", "magical",
"sparkly". This product handles other people's livelihoods.

## Typography direction

- **Do not** use Inter, Instrument Serif, Instrument Sans, DM Sans, DM Serif,
  Plus Jakarta Sans, Space Grotesk, Space Mono, Outfit, Syne, Playfair,
  Fraunces, Cormorant, Newsreader, Lora, Crimson, IBM Plex *. Every one of
  these is on the impeccable reflex-reject list — they create monoculture.
- **Consider** instead: Söhne, National 2, Neue Haas Grotesk, Neue Haas
  Unica, GT America, ABC Arizona, ABC Diatype, ABC Oracle, Pangram Sans,
  Migra, Untitled Sans, Söhne Mono. Paid faces are acceptable; licensing
  lives in `docs/typography-licenses.md` (to be authored) when we pick.
- Body typography must hit 65–75ch line length and have real leading.
- Use at least a 1.25 modular scale. Numbers in matter metadata use tabular
  figures (`font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums`).

## Colour direction

- **OKLCH only.** Neutrals are tinted toward a cool indigo — the ink of
  court-room pens, not the blue of SaaS dashboards.
- 60-30-10 weight: ink and surface neutrals dominate, secondary line and
  mute tones carry structure, a single accent marks primary actions and
  verified citations. Accent is rare; overusing it kills it.
- **No** cyan-on-dark, purple-to-blue gradients, neon, or glow. The default
  theme is light; a dark theme is a separate workstream gated by tenant
  preference (not shipped yet).

## Information density

- Dense by default. Cards are allowed but not mandatory — data tables beat
  card grids for matter portfolios, invoices, counsel, recommendations.
- Empty states are a feature, not a decoration: always explain what lands
  here, why it's empty, and what the user can do.
- Loading states use Skeleton primitives, not spinners on big surfaces.

## Interaction principles

- **Keyboard-first.** Every primary action has a keyboard path. The command
  bar lives in the topbar and is the fastest way to jump anywhere.
- **Don't hide actions behind hover on non-desktop.** iPad in court is a
  real viewport.
- **Confirmations are rare.** Undo is better than a modal. Destructive
  actions (delete matter, void invoice) confirm once, clearly.

## Accessibility floor

- WCAG 2.1 AA. All interactive elements have visible focus. No colour-only
  signals. No motion that cannot be respected via `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- Every label is associated with its input. Every dialog has a name.
- Screen readers must be able to reach the Matter Cockpit tabs and the
  Recommendations accept/reject buttons without losing context.

## What is NOT CaseOps

If a contributor finds themselves reaching for any of the below, they have
drifted away from the brief:

- Glassmorphism, neumorphism, heavy shadows, glow effects.
- Emoji in UI (reserved for user-generated content only).
- "AI gradients" (purple-to-pink, cyan-to-green).
- Big friendly illustrations in empty states.
- Playful microcopy ("Oops!", "Whoopsie", "Let's get started!").
- Animations that bounce, spring, or overshoot.

If in doubt: look at Bloomberg Terminal, the printed Supreme Court Reports,
and the cleanest bits of Linear and Vercel. Triangulate. We sit between
those worlds, not next to a consumer SaaS product.
